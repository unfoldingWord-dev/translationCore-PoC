===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Seeing their faith, Jesus said** - It is understood that they believe Jesus could heal the paralyzed man. This could be stated. AT: "When Jesus perceived that they believed that he could heal the man, he said to him." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_ellipsis]])
  * **Man** - This is a general word that people used when speaking to a man whose name they did not know. It was not rude, but it also did not show special respect. Some languages might use a word like "friend" or "sir."
  * **your sins are forgiven you** - This could be stated in active form. AT: "you are forgiven" or "I forgive your sins" (UDB). (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **question this** - "discuss this" or "reason about this." What they questioned could be stated. AT: "discuss whether or not Jesus had authority to forgive sins." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_ellipsis]])
  * **Who is this who speaks blasphemies?** - This question shows how shocked and angry they were at what Jesus said. This could be written as a statement. AT: "This man is blaspheming God" or "He blasphemes God by saying that." (See [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **Who can forgive sins, but God alone?** - The implied information is that if a person claims to forgive sins he says he is God. This could be written as a clear statement. AT: "No one can forgive sins but God alone" or "God is the only one who can forgive sins." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]], [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
