===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **General Information:** - This gives background to show why Mary and Joseph have to move for the birth of Jesus. 

  * **Now** - This word marks the beginning of a new part of the story. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_newevent]])
  * **it came about that** - This phrase is used to show that this is the beginning of an account. If your language has a way of showing the start of an account, you may use that. Some versions do not include this phrase.
  * **Caesar Augustus** - "King Augustus" or "Emperor Augustus." Augustus was the first emperor of the Roman Empire. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_participants]])
  * **sent out a decree ordering** - They idiom means he gave an order or a command. AT: "ordered" or "gave a command." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **that a census be taken of all the people living in the Roman world** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "that they register all the people living in the Roman world" or "that they count all the people in the Roman world and write down their names." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **the Roman world** - "the part of the world controlled by the Roman government" or "the countries ruled by the Roman emperor"
  * **Quirinius** - Quirinius was appointed to be the governor of Syria. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]])
  * **everyone went** - "everyone started off" or "everyone was going"
  * **his own town** - It may be helpful to state that "his own" does not refer to the town where he lived. AT: "the town in which his ancestors lived." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **to be registered for the census** - "to have their names written in the register" or "to be included in the official count"
