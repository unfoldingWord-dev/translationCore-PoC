===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Jesus compares the thoughts of a person to their good or evil treasure. The good thoughts of good people cause good actions. The evil thoughts of evil people cause evil actions. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])

  * **The good man** - The word "good" here refers to righteousness or moral goodness.
  * **good man** - The word "man" here refers to a person, male or female. AT: "good person." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_gendernotations]])
  * **the good treasure of his heart** - "the good things he keeps in his heart" or "the good things he values" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **brings out that which is good** - Bringing out treasure is a metaphor for doing actions. This could be stated. AT: "lives his life and shows that which is good" or "does the things that are good." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **that which is good** - "good things"
  * **the evil treasure of his heart** - "the evil things he keeps in his heart" or "the evil things he values" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **from the abundance of his heart his mouth speaks** - "what he thinks in his heart affects what he says with his mouth" or "what he values in his heart determines what he says with his mouth."
